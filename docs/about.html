About Me

The story behind the work

Who I Am

I’m Han (Simon) Yan, a data scientist and quantitative researcher currently pursuing a Master of Professional Studies in Information Science at Cornell University, with a focus on AI Assurance Technology.

My work sits at an unusual intersection: I care deeply about making AI systems not just smart, but trustworthy — reliable enough that a business can stake real decisions on them. That means thinking carefully about data integrity, model transparency, and the gap between a model’s performance on paper and its behavior in the wild.

Before Cornell, I worked at Central China Securities, where I built data pipelines handling hundreds of thousands of financial records daily and developed multi-factor alpha signals for equity research. That experience taught me that the unglamorous work — cleaning, validating, and stress-testing data — is often where real analytical value is created.

Academic Foundation

My quantitative backbone comes from deep study of:

  • Stochastic Processes & Markov Chains — modeling systems that evolve probabilistically over time
  • Monte Carlo Simulation — quantifying uncertainty where closed-form solutions don’t exist
  • Machine Learning — with a critical eye toward when and why models fail
  • Geospatial Analysis — visualizing socioeconomic phenomena across space and time

I believe mathematical rigor and practical engineering need each other. A model that can’t be deployed is a thought experiment. A pipeline without statistical foundations is a liability.

Beyond the Work

When I’m not in the weeds of a dataset, I’m interested in the broader questions of how quantitative tools shape decisions — in finance, in policy, and increasingly in AI governance. I’m based in Ithaca, NY, and I enjoy long walks by Cayuga Lake when the Ithaca weather cooperates (which is not always).
